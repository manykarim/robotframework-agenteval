## What didn't, and what we learned

### 1. Epic 8 retro Action #1 (cross-LLM review pipeline restoration) was NOT addressed in Epic 9

**This is the load-bearing miss of Epic 9.** Epic 8 retro identified Action #1 as the load-bearing blocker for retro continuity. The proposed falsifiable experiment had 4 steps:

(i) Re-run Story 8a.2 prompt through `claude -p --dangerously-skip-permissions --model opus` and verify >0 bytes.
(ii) Verify Codex quota state via OpenAI dashboard.
(iii) Attempt third LLM family (Gemini / Mistral CLI) as fallback.
(iv) Add in-loop circuit-breaker that aborts on ≥2 consecutive empty review artifacts.

Epic 9 closed Phase 1 (Stories 9.1, 9.2, 9.3 done) without addressing ANY of the 4 steps — characterizing the Epic 7 retro-of-retro as "step (i) implicit" is post-hoc reasoning the cross-LLM review of THIS retro rejected (Critique 3). Step (i) explicitly required re-running the **Story 8a.2 review prompt** through `claude -p ...` and verifying >0 bytes on disk; the Epic 7 retro-of-retro ran the invocation against a *different, shorter* input (a retro draft), which cannot falsify the long-prompt-specific failure-mode hypothesis the Epic 8 retro was investigating. **Action #1: 0 of 4 done.** The same gap Epic 8 retro identified continued unfixed through Epic 9. *Patched 2026-05-25 per cross-LLM review Critique 3.*

**Practical consequence:** 0 of 3 Epic 9 stories received cross-LLM review. The 30+ STAR catch streak from Epics 2-7 remains broken. Epic 8 break = **4 stories without cross-LLM review** (Story 8a.1 received a substantive Claude review — 3 HIGH + 3 MED + 3 LOW v1 — per Epic 8 retro L31; Stories 8a.2/8b.1/8b.2/8b.3 were the 4 without). Epic 9 extends the streak to **7 total stories without** (4 + 3). The integration-test forcing function (per `feedback_integration_test_forcing_function` Epic 8 retro NEW norm) caught no Epic 9 empirical bugs because Epic 9 is documentation-only — meaning the mitigation pattern WAS not tested in Epic 9 either. *Patched 2026-05-25 per cross-LLM review Critique 1 — original draft inflated to "5 + 3 = 8" by undercounting Story 8a.1's substantive review.*

**Lesson:** the autonomous `/goal` loop has a structural bias toward forward-momentum (close the next story) over retro-debt cleanup (address last retro's load-bearing action). Epic 7 retro Action #4 (`@guarded_fanout` decision) is now 5+ epics old. Epic 8 retro Action #1 is 1 epic old. **Without an explicit "block forward progress until last retro's load-bearing actions land" gate, retro debt accumulates faster than it clears.**
